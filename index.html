Duquesne Incline + Mt. Washington Overlook
🚶 Walk — right at Station Square
Pittsburgh's most iconic view — a quick, affordable incline ride up Mt. Washington for a sweeping skyline/three-rivers photo, then back down. Doesn't take long, easy with a group. Map
The Andy Warhol Museum
🚇 ~10 min via the T (then short walk)
117 Sandusky St, Pittsburgh PA 15212 · Take the T from Station Square to North Side, short walk from there · Modern art museum, very photogenic, holds attention well, all-indoor. Note: some exhibits explore mature/adult themes — parents may want to use discretion with younger teens, and museum staff can point you toward family-friendly galleries. Map
Kamin Science Center & SportsWorks
🚗 ~10 min drive
1 Allegheny Ave, Pittsburgh PA 15212 · Formerly Carnegie Science Center, renamed Kamin Science Center · Convenient to the North Shore, steps from PNC Park · Hands-on science exhibits plus SportsWorks — speed pitch, vertical leap, batting-style challenges. Especially good for athletes and teens. Free teen membership available for ages 13–18. Map

Pregame Eats Near PNC Park 🍽️
Pregame on the North Shore (best option): North Shore Tavern, Burgatory, Southern Tier, or Federal Galley food hall — all an easy walk from the gates, casual and built for groups. SoHo Pittsburgh and Legends of the North Shore are also popular pregame spots, but both fill up fast on game days — worth calling ahead or arriving right at open. PNC Park also has plenty of concessions (Primanti's stand, BBQ, classic ballpark food) if you'd rather eat inside.
